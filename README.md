## Datacenter, Residential, ISP, Mobile: What Actually Differs

Three real choices when you buy proxy USA, plus one niche category. Here's the honest breakdown.

**Datacenter proxies** are cheap, fast, and live in server farms. Latency is excellent, bandwidth is usually unlimited or generous, and prices land in the cents-per-IP range. Trade-off: they're easy to detect. Sites that rely on serious anti-bot vendors (Cloudflare's enterprise tier, DataDome, PerimeterX) will block them on harder targets.

**Residential proxies** route through real home connections—IPs assigned by Comcast, Spectrum, AT&T, Verizon to actual subscribers. From a website's view, you look like a regular person browsing from Brooklyn or Boise. Detection rates plummet. Cost climbs, usually billed per gigabyte of traffic rather than per IP.

**ISP proxies** (sometimes called static residential) are the hybrid. They're hosted in datacenters but registered under residential ISP ASNs. You get datacenter sped with residential trust signals. They're sticky, meaning the same IP stays with you across sessions, which maters for account-based work.

**Mobile proxies** route through 4G/5G carier networks. The hardest type to detect, often used for Instagram, TikTok, and bot-prone social platforms. Also the most expensive.

For most "buy proxy USA" searchers, the right answer is either residential rotating (for scraping and stealth) or ISP-static (for managing accounts that need a consistent IP). Datacenter is fine if your target sites are simple. Mobile is overkill unless you're working at the edges of social automation.
